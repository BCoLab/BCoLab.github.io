Description:

The long-term goal of our research is to identify the neural circuits underlying perceptual and cognitive functions. To this end, we are studying the activity of single and population of neurons recorded from animals that are trained to perform selected cognitive tasks. Brain’s cognitive functions emerge from computations implemented by neuronal networks. In our lab, a mixture of electrophysiological, behavioral, and computational techniques are used to understand brain as a computational organ. Our current research focuses on the following topics: neuronal mechanisms involved in spatio-temporal dynamic of object representation; role of network states in preserving complex object information; role of neuronal feedback control for object recognition during partial occlusion; and the neuromodulatory basis of cognitive functions
